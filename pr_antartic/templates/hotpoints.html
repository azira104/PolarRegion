Antarctica Hotpoints

2000-2021 Hotpoints For Investigation Climate Change

The satellite data from MODIS C6.1, SUOMI VIIRS C2, and J1 VIIRS C1 utilized for monitoring hotspots/fire activity in Antarctica between 2000 and 2021 offer key attributes including latitude, longitude, brightness temperature, acquisition date and time, satellite source, confidence level, fire radiative power (FRP), hotspot type, and day/night detection. Brightness temperatures, FRP, and hotspot types aid in identifying and categorizing fire pixels, with FRP particularly useful for estimating biomass burning contributions to carbon budgets. Confidence levels vary based on factors like sun glint and temperature anomalies, with certain regions experiencing low confidence nighttime detections due to potential false alarms. Data processing versions indicate whether the data is Near Real-Time (NRT) or Standard processed. Despite uncertainties in FRP measurements, especially on a per-pixel basis, analysis at coarser spatiotemporal resolutions can mitigate these uncertainties, enabling more reliable biomass burning estimates.
